Plain-language mechanism read

Loop1 to loop2 is the big local-repair jump: blank accuracy moves from 58.77% to 83.05%. That still barely solves full boards.

Loop2 to loop5 adds little blank accuracy, but exact keeps moving upward. That is the more interesting part: late loops are not just making more cells right; they are sometimes making the board coherent.

Failure after loop5 is therefore a whole-board coordination problem. A few coupled errors can keep exact near zero even when most hidden cells are correct.

FutureSeed reading

The h72 archive reports FutureSeed gate mean 0.540 and seed-state norm 8.639. That tells us the global seed is active, but it is not enough by itself: loop1 exact is 0.00%.

The useful story is FutureSeed plus recurrence. FutureSeed gives a noncausal global initialization; loop reuses it and iteratively cleans the board. The present archive does not store per-token seed influence, so the next visualization needs richer traces.
